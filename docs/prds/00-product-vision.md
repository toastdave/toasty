# Product Vision

## Working title

Toasty is a fun-but-serious screen-media discovery app that helps people find what to watch, rate it in a more expressive way than stars, understand their own taste, and eventually battle through yearly tournament brackets.

## Product pillars

1. Useful in under ten seconds.
2. Playful enough to invite repeat browsing.
3. Structured enough to support meaningful cross-media comparison.
4. Deep enough to support long-term collecting, rating, recommendation, and competition.

## Experience posture

The product should feel clear, warm, and trustworthy first, then distinctive in the moments that deserve it.

- baseline interaction model: Airbnb-like clarity, spacing, and calm confidence
- product flavor: tasteful charting, recommendation explanations, expressive rating outputs, and tournament energy
- avoid: generic SaaS chrome, noisy gamer UI, or novelty that reduces trust

## Launch posture

The launch surface is anime-first because it gives us a clear source strategy through Jikan, a passionate user loop, and high-value discovery pages with relatively low operational overhead.

Anime remains the flagship starting lane, but the medium-term roadmap expands into TV shows and movies. Cross-media comparison matters from the start, even before all media types are live in the product surface.

## Core product loops

### Discovery loop

Users should be able to land, browse, and understand what is worth their time immediately through charts, schedules, featured picks, and recommendation context.

### Taste loop

Users should be able to track what they want to watch, rate what they finished, and gradually build a structured taste profile.

### Recommendation loop

Toasty should move from static charts into explainable recommendations that use metadata, trend signals, tracking behavior, and rating profiles to suggest what to watch next.

### Tournament loop

Toasty should culminate in annual bracket events that turn accumulated ratings, engagement, and trend energy into a March Madness-style competition with strong archives and defensible seeding.

## MVP outcome

The first release should let a new visitor:

- browse top anime all time
- browse current airing schedules
- inspect a detail page with canonical metadata and source links
- understand season progress at a glance when the source data supports it
- see the shape of the future product through tracking, ratings, recommendations, and tournament affordances

## Long-range expansion

Once the anime vertical is healthy, the data model and service layer should expand into TV shows and movies without forcing a UI rewrite.

Books and music can remain structurally possible in the schema, but near-term product planning should stay focused on screen media.
